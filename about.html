About Me

Who am I?

Introduction:

My name is Nathaniel (I usually go by Nathan) Atkins. I'm a junior web developer, composer, songwriter, music producer, and sound designer. Although I do quite a bit in regards to audio, considering I've been doing that for about 8 years, I'm beginning to focus my attention more on being a web developer. I began my journey in web development in June of 2020. That was when I began my cohort at Eleven Fifty Academy, a school which teaches coding of all sorts, from the web development pathway that I took, to software development and even cyber security. As for my music background, I began making music back in 2012 as a hobby and have continued growing in that field ever since. I'm primarily self taught, and have improved in my abilities quite a bit over the years. If you continue reading below, I've written individual articles going over my history in both web development and music production.

Web Development

As previously mentioned, I began my adventure into web development in June of 2020. I attended Eleven Fifty Academy, which not only educated me in regards to the knowledge I know today, but also taught me to pursue more knowledge on my own. I loved to find out new methods of doing things in programming before they were even formally taught to me. If I wanted something done, I'd spend my night Googling away, asking my instructors for help, as well as asking other programmers for help on public forums and Discord servers. I felt like I couldn't rest until I figured out what I needed to figure out. One thing that helped me greatly when I began Eleven Fifty was actually the knowledge which I had attained before even beginning the course. I took it upon myself to look into Python as a way to get my feet wet before diving into my programming journey. I learned quite a bit from various online courses and video tutorials. It made it siginificantly easier to understand concepts in JavaScript, considering I had basically already learned them from using those concepts in Python. If you wish to see the projects I've completed while at Eleven Fifty, you can view them by clicking on the "Projects" tab, or by scrolling down.

Music Production
Back in 2012, just a month or so before I began high school, I decided that I wanted to pursue electronic music production. I had been into electronic music in general for several years before making this decision, and had an interest in music dating far before even that. I had some experience before beginning this adventure into music, mainly from playing clarinet in band class, but really wasn't prepared yet for the journey I'd be taking on. In all honesty, I didn't even know where I was supposed to begin. I didn't know what tools I'd need and what I'd need to learn as far as music knowledge went. So I began by simply Googling "How to make Dubstep" into Google, as an aspiring young music producer would. I was immediately met with the information I was looking for, and everything seemed like it was attainable enough at first, but then I saw the price tags. That's when I decided the best course of action was to get to work raising up some money. I ended up gaining enough money from chores (remember, I was like a freshman in high school at this point) to eventually get myself a decent enough computer to make music on, but before that even happened, something else did. Early into my journey, my wonderful parents acknowledged my drive towards wanting to make music and decided they'd get the me standard version of Ableton Live as a Christmas gift. This would turn out to be one of those coin flip moments in life that would determine my future. Immediately I began trying to work with music. I expiremented extensively with everything inside of Ableton, looked up countless videos on how to do specific things, and did a lot of Googling of terms, trying to figure out what all the knobs do. At first I didn't really have much of a grasp at music theory and instead just tried to put together notes that sounded good. After a bunch of experimenting over the course of 2 years or so, I eventually began getting halfway decent at creating music. Overtime, I continued to progress, and now after 8 years of doing this, I've gotten to where I am today.
